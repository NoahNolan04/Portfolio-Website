Hi, I'm Noah.

MSU · Games & Interactive Media B.A. Korean Seoul · Summer '25 & '26 Unity · C# Premiere Pro · CapCut

What I do

I study Games and Interactive Media at Michigan State with a focus in game design, alongside a second major in Korean. My work spans five disciplines that constantly feed each other: the sound design sharpens my video edits, the writing shapes my game narratives, and the interface design shows up in everything.

On the games side, I build in Unity with C# — from 2D platformers with tight, movement-focused mechanics to 3D shooters — and publish playable builds on itch.io. On the media side, I produce, shoot, and edit video, design and record audio stories, and write everything from esports journalism to scripts and narrative monologues.

Korea

My connection to Korea runs deeper than coursework. In the summer of 2025 I interned at Daebak, a Korean culture subscription brand in Seoul, through the CRCC Asia Global Internship Program — producing, scripting, filming, and editing the videos at the center of the company's brand revival campaign, and launching blog content on Korean culture and esports. In 2026 I returned to Seoul to study at Yonsei University. I write about the LCK, follow Korean esports closely, and am working toward professional fluency in the language.

What I'm looking for

Roles where making things is the job — game design, video editing and production, sound, or media work that sits at the intersection of a few of these. I'm especially interested in teams working across the US–Korea media and gaming space, where both halves of my degree get to work.
